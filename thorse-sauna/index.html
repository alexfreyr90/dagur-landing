The THORSE Philosophy

Why Icelandic Horse Hair?

Superior to wool in every way that matters for sauna

🌡️

Superior Heat Insulation

Icelandic horse hair grows in extreme cold (-30°C), producing fibers that are naturally thicker and denser than wool. The trapped air pockets create an insulating barrier that keeps your head cool while your body absorbs the heat.

💧

Moisture Wicking

Horse hair naturally wicks moisture away from the scalp, keeping you dry in the steamiest löyly. Unlike synthetic materials, it breathes while still insulating — the best of both worlds.

🛡️

Incredibly Durable

Horse hair is stronger than wool and resists matting over time. A THORSE hat will outlast dozens of ordinary sauna hats. The fibers maintain their structure even after hundreds of sessions.

🦠

Naturally Antibacterial

Horse hair has natural antimicrobial properties that resist odor and bacteria buildup — essential for a product used in hot, humid environments. Less washing, longer life.

🐴

Ethically Sourced

Every strand is collected during natural shedding and grooming from Icelandic farms. No horse is ever harmed. The hair is a renewable by-product of Iceland's centuries-old horse culture.

🇮🇸

Authentic Icelandic Heritage

Iceland's geothermal bathing culture is UNESCO-recognized. Our hats connect two Icelandic traditions — the thousand-year-old horse and the ancient hot spring — in one premium product.

Two Icelandic Traditions. One Hat.

Iceland's geothermal bathing culture dates back to the Viking Age. In 2025, it was recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage. With 79% of Icelanders visiting pools regularly, bathing is the nation's living room.

The Icelandic horse — unchanged for a thousand years, never crossed with other breeds — has survived volcanic winters and Arctic storms. Their hair is nature's most resilient fiber.

THORSE brings these two traditions together for the first time: the ancient art of Icelandic bathing, protected by the hair of Iceland's most iconic animal.

How It's Made

From pasture to sauna in six steps

01

Ethical Collection

Hair gathered during natural spring shedding and regular grooming from Icelandic farms. Fully traceable, zero waste.

02

Sort by Type

Tail hair (strongest, for The Viking), mane hair (softest, for The Aurora), and blended for The Classic. Each sorted by hand.

03

Wash & Prepare

Gentle cleaning with Icelandic geothermal water. No chemicals. The natural oils are preserved for water resistance.

04

Wet Felting

Traditional wet felting technique — hot water, friction, and time compress the fibers into dense, insulating felt. Each hat takes 3-4 hours to felt by hand.

05

Shaping & Finishing

Molded on wooden forms, trimmed, and finished with hand-stitched details and a leather THORSE origin tag.

06

Certificate of Origin

Every hat ships with a card naming the farm and horse it came from. From Iceland, with love.

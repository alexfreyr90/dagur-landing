Why This Works in Iceland

80K+

Icelandic Horses

Iceland has one of the highest horse-to-human ratios in the world. Hair is an abundant, renewable by-product of grooming and natural shedding — zero waste.

1,000yr

Purebred Heritage

No horses have been imported to Iceland since the 10th century. The breed is genetically unique — and their hair carries properties found nowhere else on Earth.

-30°C

Cold-Climate Superiority

Extreme cold produces thicker, stronger, more resilient hair. This is why Mongolian and Icelandic horse hair commands premium prices for violin bows and luxury textiles.

2

Competitors Worldwide

Only John Boyd (UK, est. 1837) and Le Crin (France, est. 1814) weave horsehair fabric. The market is wide open for an Icelandic brand with authentic provenance.

2.4M

Tourists Per Year

Iceland's tourism boom creates a massive retail opportunity. Horsehair jewelry and crafts as authentic Icelandic souvenirs with real story and heritage value.

85%

Jewelry Margins

Horsehair jewelry has the lowest startup cost and highest margins of any product line. The "Made in Iceland" story adds 30-50% price premium versus generic horsehair products.

Sustainable by Nature

Every strand of THORSE hair is ethically collected during natural shedding seasons and regular grooming. No horse is ever harmed. The hair is a renewable by-product of Iceland's centuries-old horse culture — transforming waste into luxury.

We work directly with Icelandic farms, ensuring full traceability from pasture to product. Each item comes with a certificate of origin.

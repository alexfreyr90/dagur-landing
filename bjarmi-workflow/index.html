01
ETHICAL COLLECTION
Icelandic Horse Farms | ~1 day per farm visit

Hair is collected during natural spring shedding season (April-May) and regular grooming sessions. Icelandic horses grow exceptionally thick winter coats due to extreme cold (-30C), producing dense, resilient fibers ideal for felting.

  • Tail hair: Strongest fibers, 30-90cm, used for The Viking hat
  • Mane hair: Softer fibers, 15-30cm, used for The Aurora hat
  • Body/undercoat: Short dense fibers, 3-8cm, ideal base for felting
  • Each horse yields 200-400g of usable hair per season
QC CHECKPOINT: Farm traceability certificate issued. Each batch tagged with farm name, horse ID, and collection date.
02
SORTING AND GRADING
Skilled Manual Process | ~45 min per 500g batch

Raw hair is hand-sorted strand by strand using steel-pin hackles. Fibers are separated by type, length, thickness, and color. Damaged or weak strands are removed.

  • Grade A (tail, 30cm+): Premium - The Viking
  • Grade B (mane, 15-30cm): Standard - The Aurora
  • Grade C (mixed/body): Blended - The Classic Eldfjall
  • Reject rate: ~15-20% of raw hair removed during sorting
QC CHECKPOINT: Batch grading report. Each grade weighed and logged. Manual fiber strength pull test.
03
WASHING AND CLEANING
Geothermal Water Process | ~2 hours

Sorted hair is washed in naturally heated Icelandic geothermal water at 38-42C. A mild, biodegradable soap removes dust and excess oils while preserving natural coating for water resistance.

  • First wash: Warm geothermal water + mild soap, 20 min agitation
  • Rinse: Clean geothermal water, repeated 2-3 times until clear
  • No chemical treatments, bleaches, or synthetic detergents
  • Natural oils preserved for moisture-wicking properties
QC CHECKPOINT: Visual clarity test on rinse water. pH test. Fiber flexibility check.
04
DRYING
Controlled Air Drying | 24-48 hours

Washed hair is spread thinly on mesh drying racks in a ventilated room (18-22C). Must be completely dry before felting. Turned every 6-8 hours for even drying.

  • Spread to max 1cm thickness on mesh racks
  • Room maintained at 40-50% humidity
  • No heat dryers - artificial heat damages fiber structure
  • Weight checked against wet weight to confirm drying
QC CHECKPOINT: Moisture content verified below 8%. Visual check for mold.
05
CARDING AND PREPARATION
Fiber Alignment | ~30 min per hat batch

Dried hair is picked (fluffed) by hand, then carded to create uniform batts of ~200g per hat. Cross-layering ensures strength in all directions.

  • Hand-picking separates clumps and mixes fibers
  • Carding creates uniform batts of ~200g per hat
  • 4-6 layers with alternating fiber direction
  • Cross-layering ensures multi-directional strength
QC CHECKPOINT: Batt weight verified at 200g. Visual uniformity inspection.
06
WET FELTING
Core Manufacturing Step | 3-4 hours per hat

The critical transformation. Layered batts are placed over a hat-shaped resist, wetted with hot water (55-65C) and soap. Artisan agitates until keratin scales interlock permanently, creating dense felt.

  • Phase 1 - Pre-felt (30 min): Gentle rubbing
  • Phase 2 - Fulling (90-120 min): Vigorous rolling and kneading
  • Phase 3 - Hardening (60 min): Hot water + rolling for density
  • Felt shrinks 30-40% from original size
  • Target density: 0.25-0.30 g/cm3
QC CHECKPOINT: Thickness at 5 points (5.5-7mm). Density check. No thin spots.
07
SHAPING AND MOLDING
Hat Form Creation | ~1 hour + overnight

Felted body shaped over carved wooden hat block specific to each model. Wet felt stretched, smoothed, pinned. Dries overnight taking permanent shape.

  • Eldfjall (Classic): Tall dome, no brim, 18cm crown
  • Thor (Viking): Wide 4cm brim, structured, 16cm height
  • Nordurljos (Aurora): Soft dome, slight roll brim, 15cm
  • Wooden blocks hand-carved from Icelandic birch
QC CHECKPOINT: Dimensional check against model spec. Shape symmetry.
08
FINISHING AND DETAILING
Hand Finishing | ~1.5 hours per hat

Edges trimmed with precision scissors. Surface steamed and brushed. Hand-stitched reinforcement at stress points. Leather Bjarmi origin tag attached.

  • Edge trimming with curved scissors
  • Light steam pressing to smooth surface
  • Double-stitch reinforcement at crown/brim (Thor)
  • Leather tag hand-stitched inside
  • Gold accent thread on Thor model
QC CHECKPOINT: Visual inspection. Edge evenness. Stitch quality.
09
QUALITY CONTROL
Final Verification | ~20 min per hat

Every hat: dimensional verification, heat tolerance testing (95C steam), insulation measurement, aesthetic review. Failed hats recycled into raw material.

  • Dimensional check at 5 points
  • Heat test: 2-min at 95C steam
  • Insulation: thermal camera measurement
  • Aesthetic: color, smoothness, symmetry
  • Pass rate target: 90%+
QC CHECKPOINT: QC certificate with serial number. Pass/fail logged.
10
PACKAGING AND SHIPPING
Premium Unboxing | ~10 min per unit

Linen drawstring bag inside branded kraft box. Certificate of Origin (farm, horse, artisan). Care instructions. Sealed with Bjarmi wax stamp.

  • Linen storage bag
  • Branded kraft box with gold stamp
  • Certificate of Origin with farm and horse name
  • Care card with lifetime guarantee info
  • Worldwide tracked shipping from Reykjavik
QC CHECKPOINT: Final checklist: hat, bag, certificate, care card, seal.

Why Icelandic Horse Hair?

Superior to wool in every way that matters for sauna

🌡️

Superior Heat Insulation

Icelandic horse hair grows in extreme cold (-30°C), producing fibers that are naturally thicker and denser than wool. The trapped air pockets keep your head cool while your body absorbs the heat.

💧

Moisture Wicking

Horse hair naturally wicks moisture away from the scalp. Unlike synthetics, it breathes while insulating — keeping you dry in the steamiest löyly.

🛡️

Incredibly Durable

Horse hair is stronger than wool and resists matting. A Bjarmi hat outlasts dozens of ordinary sauna hats. The fibers hold their structure after hundreds of sessions.

🦠

Naturally Antibacterial

Natural antimicrobial properties resist odor and bacteria — essential for hot, humid environments. Less washing, longer life.

🐴

Ethically Sourced

Every strand is collected during natural shedding and grooming from Icelandic farms. No horse is ever harmed. A renewable by-product of centuries-old horse culture.

🇮🇸

UNESCO Heritage

Iceland's geothermal bathing culture is UNESCO-recognized. Bjarmi connects the thousand-year-old horse with the ancient hot spring tradition.

Two Traditions. One Hat.

Iceland's geothermal bathing culture dates back to the Viking Age. In 2025, it was recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage. With 79% of Icelanders visiting pools regularly, bathing is the nation's living room.

The Icelandic horse — unchanged for a thousand years — has survived volcanic winters and Arctic storms. Their hair is nature's most resilient fiber.

Bjarmi brings these traditions together: the ancient art of Icelandic bathing, protected by the hair of Iceland's most iconic animal.

How It's Made

From pasture to sauna in six steps

01

Ethical Collection

Hair gathered during natural spring shedding from Icelandic farms. Fully traceable, zero waste.

02

Sort by Type

Tail hair for The Viking, mane hair for The Aurora, blended for The Classic. Each sorted by hand.

03

Wash & Prepare

Gentle cleaning with Icelandic geothermal water. No chemicals. Natural oils preserved for water resistance.

04

Wet Felting

Traditional wet felting — hot water, friction, and time compress the fibers into dense, insulating felt. 3-4 hours per hat.

05

Shaping & Finishing

Molded on wooden forms, trimmed, and finished with hand-stitched details and a leather Bjarmi origin tag.

06

Certificate of Origin

Every hat ships with a card naming the farm and horse it came from. From Iceland, with love.
